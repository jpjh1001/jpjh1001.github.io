What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I think this bootcamp made myself more humble. I say this because I use up to sprint 3, I was somewhat able to submit my work on time as I could understand what I was doing. Hoever, during sprint 4 and 5, I struggle so much that I had quite a few mental breakdowns. So these core reflections made me to reflect on how I was approaching the content.

2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think the role of my values were used as guidelines to which I followed. When things got tough (it was really tough for me during sprint 4 and 5) it was very important for me to ask myself the question, 'why did I start this in the first place?' This question was a good motivator to keep me going while learning the complex concepts.

The role of empathy was that when things got tough, I was able to receive more support than I was able to give to my peers which I think was really important to those people who weren't that accustomed to studying online.

The role of self-awareness was to realise the current state I was in. To ask myself the questions, am I stuck? Or am I too hard on myself? These questions helped me to balance my stress levels during foundations. What helped me a lot when I was struggling was once again my tutors and my peers for encouraging me by giving me postiive messages and offering help when I was at need. Even when I was asking for help at a very late time of the day.

5. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it's because as mentioned by Dev Academy that employers are looking not only at an individuals coding ability but they also place a high emphasis on their interactions with people. The core learning makes you reflect on a concept and to answer the questions as if I was explaining to somebody. I think this is why we were made to practice pair programming as programming is not only about understanding the concept for yourself but to be able to write it so that it's understandable to others.
